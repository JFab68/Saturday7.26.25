Praxis Initiative is Arizona's only directly impacted-led 501(c)(3) nonprofit dedicated to transforming the criminal legal system through independent prison oversight, overdose prevention, harm reduction, arts programming, and civics training for returning citizens.

Meet Our Team

The people powering our mission for change. Driven by experience, united by purpose.

John Fabricius

Executive Director
John endured fifteen years of incarceration spanning nine distinct prison complexes, acquiring deep institutional knowledge of security operations, administrative processes, and inmate services. He channels this lived expertise into visionary reform strategies, driving legislative advocacy efforts and empowering community-driven oversight initiatives aimed at transforming Arizona's criminal legal system from the inside out.

J

Drug Policy & Substance Use Advocate
J served five years incarcerated over three convictions, gaining firsthand comprehension of carceral policies, systemic barriers, and rehabilitative challenges. Recognized for leadership in drug policy and substance use advocacy, she leverages her lived experience to shape Praxis Initiative's programs, inform policy development, and galvanize stakeholders toward humane, effective reforms within Arizona's criminal legal landscape.

Jessica

Legislative Advocate
Jessica spent eight years at the Perryville Complex, navigating all custody levels as both inmate and advocate, which forged her profound understanding of institutional shortcomings and reform opportunities. She transforms personal adversity into strategic advocacy, spearheading legislative initiatives and coalition-building efforts designed to improve conditions, expand oversight, and ensure accountability across Arizona's correctional institutions.

Sherri

Family Member Advocate
Sherri witnessed both her sons' incarcerations—one serving a thirty-year sentence and the other tragically lost to suicide after enduring deplorable conditions—which fueled her unwavering commitment to prison oversight. Drawing on personal tragedy, she courageously champions independent monitoring, legislative reforms, and community engagement to demand transparency, justice, and humane treatment for individuals within Arizona's prison system.

Mindi Krasinski

Institutional Expert
Mindi dedicated twenty-six years to ADC service, rising from administrative typist to Correctional Officer III working primarily at the Eyman, Florence and Globe complexes. Her extensive operational expertise spans security protocols, case management, and special management units. She now applies this deep institutional insight to guide Praxis Initiative's accountability measures, training programs, and policy recommendations.

Why Praxis Matters

Our Mission

Transforming the Criminal Legal System

Praxis Initiative advocates for independent, external oversight of Arizona's prison system to ensure transparency, accountability, and the protection of human rights through a fully empowered correctional ombudsman.

Independent Prison Oversight

Advocating for a fully empowered correctional ombudsman to ensure transparency and accountability.

Drug Policy & Overdose Prevention

Advancing evidence‑based policies and providing community trainings to reduce overdose deaths.

Arts in Prison & Think Motion

Delivering music, visual arts, dance, and movement workshops inside correctional facilities.

❓ What is Praxis Initiative?

Praxis Initiative is Arizona's only directly impacted-led 501(c)(3) nonprofit dedicated to transforming the criminal legal system by reducing mass incarceration, promoting transparency, and ensuring equal treatment under the law through independent prison oversight, harm reduction, and bipartisan policy solutions.
